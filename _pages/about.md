## Research Interests
Advanced control systems and hybrid dynamical systems. Modelling, analysis and control of nonlinear and power electronic systems. Currently working on data-driven and physics-informed control methodologies with applications to renewable energy and microgrids.

## Brief Bio

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics at the <a href="https://www.lagh-univ.dz" target="_blank">University of Amar Telidji, Laghouat</a>, Algeria (Member, IEEE). I received my Ph.D. in Control Engineering in 2017, under the joint supervision of <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=BQDkUg8AAAAJ" target="_blank">Prof. Atallah Benalia</a> (University of Amar Telidji, Laghouat) and <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=ApP9SGcAAAAJ" target="_blank">Prof. Mohammed Djemai</a> (ENSEA-Cergy Paris, France).

Following my Ph.D., I held a Postdoctoral Fellowship at <a href="https://www.ec-nantes.fr/" target="_blank">École Centrale de Nantes</a>, France (2017–2018), and a Research Associate position at the <a href="https://www.cam.ac.uk/" target="_blank">University of Cambridge</a>, U.K. (2018–2019), working with <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=AXz9SEoAAAAJ" target="_blank">Prof. Ioannis Lestas</a> on an ERC-funded project on analysis and control of large-scale networks, focusing on decentralized stability and performance conditions with applications to power systems and smart grids.

My research interests include **advanced control systems**, **nonlinear dynamics**, **renewable energy technologies**, **power electronics**, and **microgrids**. Current topics include: Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), Digital Twin for Power Electronics, Active Disturbance Rejection Control, Hardware-in-the-Loop simulation, and Biomedical Control Systems.
